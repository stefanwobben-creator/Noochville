# Nooch Welcome Flow — full emails

Evergreen onboarding for new subscribers (not the delay-saga; that's a separate existing-customer track).
Founder-led, English, roughly one email every 2-3 days. Voice: earthy, patient, honest, cool not hype.
No exclamation marks, no em-dashes, no unproven biodegradable / zero-waste / carbon claims.

Vision in one line: turn a subscriber into a fan and a fan into a batch member through dialogue, not
broadcast. Founders hold the vision and stay reachable; the material crew takes over once someone has
reserved. The wait is the feature, not the apology.

## Email 2 — The why
**Subject:** If Mother Earth ran a shoe company

Hi there,

You might be wondering why, of all things, we chose shoes.

Here's the short version. Every shoe carries a story about how it was made, and the industry's
version of that story is uglier than most people imagine. We stopped waiting for someone in charge
to fix it, and asked a simpler question instead. If Mother Earth ran a shoe company, what would she
make?

The brief writes itself. Start over. Protect every living thing you can. Waste nothing.

So we rolled up our sleeves and got to work, imperfectly, one step at a time. Not because we think
we're saints, but because the world does not get better on its own, and someone has to go first.

Next time, we'll show you what we were actually up against. What's really inside a normal shoe.

With love,
Lotte, Stefan and the whole Nooch crew

## Email 3 — The problem, and the contrast
**Subject:** What's actually inside a normal shoe

Hi there,

Pick up an average sneaker and you're holding petroleum. The sole is synthetic rubber, the upper is
plastic, the glue is a cocktail of chemistry, and the whole thing is built to last one season and
then sit in the ground for a few hundred years.

They call that normal. We saw a design problem.

So we grew a different shoe. The sole is natural rubber from plants, not oil. The upper is made from
things like hemp, flax and mushroom, not plastic pretending to be leather. The ink is soy. Every
material is chosen to return to nature rather than linger in it.

Same job. Opposite story.

We're still testing exactly how it all breaks down at the end, and we will only ever claim what we
can prove. But the direction is not in doubt.

Next time, the four lines we decided we'd never cross.

With love,
Lotte, Stefan and the whole Nooch crew

## Email 4 — The non-negotiables
**Subject:** Four lines we'll never cross

Hi there,

When we mapped out the dream with Mother Earth as our guide, we drew four lines. Cross one, and it
isn't a Nooch anymore.

Plants, not petroleum. Made from things that grow. No animal products, no plastic.

Planet-safe. Chosen to return to nature, not linger in it. We're testing exactly how, and we'll
claim only what we can prove.

Made with care. Produced in Europe, under European labour law, with a supply chain we can actually
see into.

Made to order. We grow only what's wanted, so nothing is wasted and nothing sits in a warehouse
pretending to be in demand.

None of this is the cheap way, or the fast way. It's just the way we're willing to do it.

Next time, why that last line means you'll wait, and why the waiting is the best part.

With love,
Lotte, Stefan and the whole Nooch crew

## Email 5 — Why the wait
**Subject:** Why we make you wait

Hi there,

Have you ever wondered how long a redwood takes to grow? The tallest trees alive, Hyperion and its
neighbours, began as seeds smaller than your fingernail and spent centuries becoming what they are.

We won't pretend a shoe takes centuries. But it does take time, and we've stopped apologising for that.

Made to order means your Noochies don't exist yet. Nothing is sitting in a warehouse. When enough
people join a batch, the materials get gathered, your pair gets made, and then it comes to you. That
is slower than a click and a next-day van. It is also the whole point: nothing wasted, nothing
overproduced, nothing made on the guess that you'd want it.

Fast fashion grows nothing and buries everything. We'd rather grow slowly and bury nothing.

Your patience is the soil this grows from.

Next time, meet the crew your shoes are actually made of.

With love,
Lotte, Stefan and the whole Nooch crew

## Email 7 — The invitation
**Subject:** How joining a batch works

Hi there,

A Nooch order isn't really a checkout. It's a reservation. Here's the honest mechanics, so nothing
surprises you.

You reserve your pair by ordering and paying up front. That pre-payment is what lets us start a batch.
If enough people join, we gather the materials and the shoes get made. If we don't reach the number
we need, you get every cent back. No batch, no risk.

We make in batches and deliver twice a year, in spring and autumn. That's slower than you're used to,
and by now you know why we think that's a feature, not a flaw.

And when your batch lands, we don't just ship a box. We throw a pick-up party, so the first time your
Noochies meet your feet is something we get to share.
