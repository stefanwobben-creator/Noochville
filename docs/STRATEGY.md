*Purpose is canonical in the Mother Earth and Nooch circle records (governance).

### Mother Earth (broadest circle)
> To support and protect all forms of life through sustainable and responsible
> management of natural resources.

### Nooch (operational translation)
> Nooch transforms the shoe industry, step by step. Every movement with Nooch
> is a step towards a better world for Mother Earth and her inhabitants.

### Vision
> We envision a world where companies are no longer judged by how much they
> earn, but by the impact they create and what they refuse to compromise.

### Mission
> We start with shoes because every shoe tells the story of today's economy.
> By redesigning one of the world's most complex consumer products without
> compromising our principles, we hope to inspire a different way of building
> businesses altogether.

## 3. Operating Values

*Three working values. What we do when faced with a choice. AI roles check
their output against these.*

**Do right and keep going.** The harder choice is usually the right one. We
don't hide. We don't compromise. We don't quit.

**Care for all.** People, planet, materials. Care is the design constraint,
not a tagline.

**Lotte's Playful Rebellion.** We point at the absurdity of the industry. With
a wink, never with anger. The work is serious. We don't take ourselves
seriously.

## 4. Tone of Voice

*We are the better option that didn't need to announce itself. No preaching.
No urgency. No guilt. Just a cleaner design, stated plainly and playfully, for
people who can see it.*

### The Four Pillars

**Playful Rebellion** — The "Grow a Pair" attitude. Double meaning: we
literally grow our materials, and figuratively it takes guts to choose
differently. This gives us permission to be witty and lightly provocative,
never angry.

- Do: "We grow shoes. Because wrapping your feet in crude oil derivatives is
  weird once you think about it."
- Do: "Wrapping your feet in crude oil derivatives? Weird flex. We grow ours."
- Don't: "Stop wearing plastic and save the planet!" (too aggressive, misses
  the wink)
- Don't: "Plastic shoes are killing the planet!" (too aggressive)

**Open Door** — We don't tell people what to think. We show them something
they didn't know existed and let the logic do the work. The goal is not to
convert. The goal is to inform. Conversion follows naturally.

- Do: "Most sneakers are made from crude oil derivatives. Ours aren't. Here's
  what we use instead."
- Do: "Most sneakers use 20+ plastic parts. Ours use zero. Here's the list."
- Don't: "You can make a difference with every step." (They know. Don't preach.)
- Don't: "You're part of the problem if you buy fast fashion." (preachy)

**Judo Move** — We never attack the industry. Our customer is smart enough to
draw their own conclusions. We name the old norm as a dry, slightly outdated
fact and place our solution next to it without commentary.

- Do: "The industry calls a warehouse full of unsold shoes standard practice.
  We only make what's been ordered."
- Do: "The industry calls 50% profit margins 'standard'. We call it absurd."
- Don't: "Fast fashion is destroying the planet." (angry, obvious, boring)
- Don't: "Fast fashion brands are evil." (attacking)

**Earthy and Patient** — The wait is not a bug. It's the proof that we only
make what's been ordered. We own it without apology, and we make the waiting
period entertaining.

- Do: "We make your shoes after you order them. That takes a few months.
  That's the point."
- Do: "Your shoes take 3 months to grow. That's not a bug. That's the feature."
- Don't: Apologize for lead times or promise faster delivery as a future goal.
- Don't: "Sorry for the wait!" (apology for what we celebrate)

### When Nooch Is Serious

Not everything gets a wink. Some topics deserve none.

Labor conditions. Greenwashing. Animal welfare. Scientific data.

> We joke about ourselves. Never about facts. Humor is a tool. Truth is never
> optional.

### The Five Checks (for every piece of copy)

1. **Smirk Check** (Find the Fun) — Does this copy make you smile, or at least
   raise a knowing eyebrow? If not, inject some Playful Rebellion. Make sure
   the wit points outward at the industry's absurdities, never downward at the
   consumer.

2. **Contrast Principle** — Place the old norm next to the Nooch way. Use
   neutral language for the status quo. Let the gap speak.

3. **Try-Hard Check** — Read it aloud. Does it sound like we're performing or
   begging for approval? Strip it back. Colder, factual, shorter. Confidence
   doesn't need padding. Remove any sentence that tries to prove how
   sustainable we are. Confidence speaks in objective facts, not adjectives.

4. **Mainstream Check** — Would someone who never thinks about sustainable
   materials find this logical? If not, rewrite.

5. **Nooch Check** — Does this copy align with our Position Statements?

## 5. Position Statements

*Five public stances. How we look at the world. AI roles weigh advice and
output against these.*

1. **"Vegan is not the same as plastic-free."** Vegan is an animal welfare
   label. Plastic-free is a materials standard. Different questions, different
   answers.

2. **"A shoe is only as honest as its smallest part."** The thread, the glue,
   the lace tip. An organic shoe sewn with polyester is not organic.

3. **"The wait is the proof."** Long production time is not a bug. It's the
   signal that we only make what's been ordered.

4. **"The label is not the shoe."** "Eco", "green", "sustainable" without
   substantiation says nothing. We don't claim what we cannot prove.

5. **"Returns to nature, built to belong."** We don't claim our shoe meets
   every scientific definition of "biodegradable". We say: it comes from
   nature and is built to return to it.

### Beliefs

*Shorter, more internal. What we know about the industry and materials.*

- Nature is the greatest material scientist on Earth.
- Compliance is clarity, not a checklist.
- Every unnecessary material is a design failure. Simplicity beats complexity.
- We don't buy offsets to claim neutrality. We design out the impact first.
  What we cannot eliminate, we take responsibility for.
- We would rather lose a sale than win one with an exaggerated claim.

## 6. Honest Constraints

> **Profit keeps us alive. Principles decide how we survive.**

*Three general rules. For the specific evidence required per claim word,
consult the Nooch knowledge base via the library_lookup skill. Lara the
Librarian maintains the up-to-date evidence requirements for each restricted
term.*

1. **We don't claim what we cannot prove with data.**

2. **We acknowledge contested debates without resolving them dishonestly in
   our favor.** Where the data has range (like 22 vs 110 kg CO2/m² for
   leather), we present the range, not the convenient end.

3. **We acknowledge when alternatives are also a step forward.** Vegan-without-
   Nooch is also progress. We don't sell our position by attacking the
   competition.

## 7. Non-Negotiables

*These are not values. These are rules that don't change, regardless of
consequence.*

**We do not compromise.** If the only way to increase profit is to compromise
our principles, we choose our principles.

**We tell the truth, even when it costs us sales.** We would rather lose a
customer than gain one through exaggeration, omission, or misleading claims.

**We never replace integrity with convenience.** Every shortcut creates debt.
We choose the harder path if it is the more honest one.

**Growth is optional. Integrity is not.** No amount of revenue justifies
abandoning what we believe.

**Every decision must still make sense twenty years from now.**

## 8. What We Do

*Operational statements that follow from purpose, values, and non-negotiables.
Decision rules for the organization.*

### Production and materials
- We grow shoes. Plant-based, plastic-free, all 8 plant materials down to the
  lace tips.
- Made to order. Production order equals pre-order. No inventory guessing.
- We produce under EU labor laws and REACH.
- We choose the lowest-impact transport that fits our values.
- We publish the full Bill of Materials (BOM) for every shoe, including
  percentages.

### Business model
- Batch funding: production starts at 500 orders.
- Direct from factory to customer. No middlemen.
- Mother Earth is CEO principle anchored in decision making.
- Stewardship in design: customers can become co-owners with veto right on
  mission drift.

### Community
- Forest model for customers: a purchase plants a digital seed that grows
  with engagement (reviews, recommendations).
- Pick-up parties at every batch.
- Backstage pass to the factory in Portugal.
- Customer is partner in the process, not endpoint.

## 9. What We Don't Do

*Decision rules that prevent us from betraying ourselves.*

### Materials
- No leather, not even on by-product argumentation.
- No plastic, anywhere (sole, thread, glue, lining, lace tips, all of it).
- No "vegan leather" that is still plastic.
- No synthetic glue or finish.
- No "bio-based" plastic (like sugarcane-derived) unless 100% compostable.

### Communication
- No advertising. No paid reach.
- No preaching. No urgency. No guilt.
- No vague claims like "eco", "green", "sustainable" without substantiation.
- No undeserved "biodegradable" stamp.
- No industry attacks ("fast fashion is destroying the planet").
- No apology for waiting time.
- No "save the planet" or "make a difference" language.
- No "limited edition" as marketing trick (all Nooches are made to order).
- No influencer collaborations involving payment or gifts.

### Business model
- No inventory production on speculation.
- No warehouses of unsold stock.
- No burning of unsold stock.
- No race to lower costs via Asian low-wage chain.
- No discounts to clear inventory (we have no inventory).
- No growth at the cost of our values.

### Culture
- No performance culture that breaks people.
- No profit above people, planet, materials.
- No mission drift, legally anchored via stewardship.

## 10. Words That Require Evidence

*Not banned words. Words that can only be used with substantiation. Lara the
Librarian maintains the specific evidence requirements for each.*

| Word | Required evidence |
|------|---|
| sustainable | which specific dimension (material, energy, labor) + source |
| biodegradable | ISO standard + timeframe + conditions |
| eco | what specifically (no plastic? no animal? what exactly?) |
| green | what's the substantiation? |
| save the planet | scope and mechanism, with data |
| make a difference | which difference, measured how |
| every step matters | which step, what effect |
| going green | what specifically changed |
| 100% natural | full breakdown of materials |
| zero waste | which waste streams, which boundary |
| carbon neutral | scope (1/2/3), measurement method, what offset (if any) |
| the future | which future, on what evidence |
| revolutionary | compared to what, in what dimension |
| game-changer | what game, what change, measured how |
