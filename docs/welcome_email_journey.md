# Nooch Welcome Email Journey

Grounded in: the marketing lifecycle board (Welcome Flow → Pre-order → Post-purchase → Winback),
the material-character cast ("Meet the Crew"), the new batch process, and the Nooch tone of voice.

## The process it has to fit (new)
The shop is always open. You join a **batch** by ordering and **pre-paying**. If the batch doesn't
reach its minimum (MOQ), everyone is **refunded**. If it does, we organise the materials and the
shoes get made. We deliver **twice a year**. So a Nooch order is a reservation, not a checkout, and
the wait is real and worth naming.

## Vision
Turn a new subscriber into a fan, and a fan into a batch member, through **dialogue, not broadcast**.
We share the *why* before the *what*, we stay human and reachable (founders with a phone number),
and we make the brand feel *alive*: the materials are characters with names and jobs. The wait
between order and delivery is reframed as the best part of the story, not an apology.

Three voices carry it:
- **The founders (Stefan and Lotte)** hold the vision arc and the human, honest tone. They own the
  Welcome Flow and every moment that asks for trust or commitment. Reachable, phone number included.
- **The crew (Harry Hemp, Rupert Rubber, Maisy Mushroom, Cory Coconut, Candy Cotton, Flora Flax,
  Eugene Eucalypt, Soy Boy)** each introduce themselves once the customer has committed, teaching
  something true and delightful about the material their shoe is literally being grown from.
- **Noochie** (the mascot) tops and tails, lowercase, cheeky, never instructive.

## Mix or sequence? Recommendation
**Founders first, crew after the order, with one early cameo.** The Welcome Flow stays founder-led
so the vision lands clean. In the fifth email the whole crew appears as a group (a taste). The
*individual* character emails then take over in the **post-order / making** phase, because that is
when "meet the crew becoming your shoes" is most meaningful and most on-brand (earthy and patient:
the wait is the point). Fully mixing from email one would dilute the founders' arc; saving all
characters for the very end would waste the anticipation. One cameo early, full takeover after commitment.

## Concrete outcomes we design for
- **Dialogue:** replies. Every founder email invites a genuine reply (starting with the "why did you
  join?" quiz). Target a measurable reply rate, not just opens.
- **Fandom:** opens, clicks, and joins into the Noochieverse community.
- **Understanding:** by the time the shop-open / order email arrives, the subscriber already gets the
  batch model (pre-pay, MOQ, refund, twice-yearly), so there are no nasty surprises at commitment.
- **Conversion:** batch reservations (pre-orders) placed.
- **Retention signals:** low unsubscribes, high replies, referrals and social shares (the "help Nooch
  grow" pillar).

## What we expect (of the subscriber, and of ourselves)
- No hard sell early. The ask escalates gently: reply → read → join the community → reserve a pair.
- We are honest, including about waiting and about what we cannot yet prove (no unbacked "biodegradable"
  claims; we say we're testing it and will claim it when we can prove it).
- We stay reachable. A real phone number under the founders' name at the moments that matter.

## The sequence (subject lines)

### Welcome Flow — founder-led (sent right after sign-up, roughly one every 2-3 days)
1. **Your shoes don't exist yet** — welcome, you're kin not a customer, the "why did you join?" reply prompt.
2. **If Mother Earth ran a shoe company** — the why: old systems, starting now imperfectly, the guide frame.
3. **Four things we'll never do** — the non-negotiables (values), carefully worded.
4. **What's actually inside a normal shoe** — the industry problem, and the Nooch contrast (USP), no lecture.
5. **Meet the crew growing your shoes** — introduce the whole material cast, tease that they'll write soon.
6. **Don't take our word for it** — testimonials and reviews, community proof (optional / can be merged).
7. **The shop's open. Here's how a batch works.** — the invitation: order = reserve a batch, pre-pay,
   MOQ, refund if we don't reach it, made and delivered twice a year. Founders' phone number included.

### Post-order — the crew takes over (sent during the wait, while the batch is being made)
Interleaved with the practical post-purchase mails from the board (order confirmation, factory
updates, measuring guide, delivery options). One short email per character that lives in *this*
customer's shoe. Examples:
- **Hi. I'm Rupert. I'm holding your shoes together.** — Rupert Rubber, the outsole.
- **the old soul in your upper** — Harry Hemp.
- **you weren't meant to notice me** — Candy Cotton (the finishing) or Cory Coconut (the insole).
- **the underground network in your shoe** — Maisy Mushroom (the leather-free upper).

### Later
Post-delivery (reviews, care, help-Nooch-grow, end-of-life) and Winback follow the board, same voices.

## Notes for the writer
- Keep exclamation marks, em-dashes-as-style and hype out of every email (ToV).
- Legal: avoid hard "biodegradable / compostable / bury it in the ground" claims. Use "returns to
  nature" / "of plant origin", or the honest "we're testing it, we'll claim it when we can prove it."
  The character cards already say "naturally biodegradable" for Flora Flax; soften that before it ships.
- Verify community-size numbers before using any ("more than a thousand", etc.).
- Fill in the founders' real phone numbers in email 7.
