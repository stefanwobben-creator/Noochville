# Nooch Crew Emails — the material characters

Sent in the post-order / making phase, while the batch is being built, interleaved with the practical
post-purchase mails. Each character introduces itself, teaches something true about the material and
where it lives in the shoe, and hands off to the next. In practice, send the characters that live in
*that* customer's pair and adjust the handoff line.

Voice: lowercase, dry, few words, one-liners. No exclamation marks, no em-dashes. Pesters Big Sneaker
without souring. Ends on an invitation, never an instruction. Legal: no hard "biodegradable" claims.

Relay order: Harry → Maisy → Rupert → Cory → Flora → Candy → Eugene → Soy Boy.

---

## 1. Harry Hemp — the upper
**Subject:** the old soul in your upper

hi. harry here. hemp.

you've got a bit of me in your uppers. the strong, quiet part.

people get nervous about my name. relax. i won't do anything except outlast most things you own. i've
been clothing humans for about ten thousand years. sails, rope, the first paper, the first jeans. i
was low-impact before anyone needed a word for it.

big sneaker skipped me for plastic. faster, cheaper, gone in a year. their call.

i grow fast, drink little, and leave the field better than i found it. then i get to be your shoe.
not a bad life.

maisy's next. she'll tell you she's the future. she's not wrong.

grow slow, wear long,
harry

---

## 2. Maisy Mushroom — the leather-free upper
**Subject:** the part of your shoe that used to be an animal

hi. maisy. mushroom.

the soft part of your upper, the bit that feels like leather? that's me. no cow involved. nobody had
to die for your shoes to feel good.

here's what people miss about mushrooms: the mushroom is just the tip. underneath i'm a network. thin
white threads, mycelium, running through the soil, linking tree to tree, passing food and messages
between them. forests lean on me. i hold the ground together.

grow those same threads in a tray, press them, and you get a material that binds, shapes and wears
like leather. that's the upper you're waiting on.

big sneaker calls me "the future of materials." sweet. i've been running forests since long before
anyone thought to sell a shoe. i'm not the future. i'm just finally getting the credit.

leather, grown instead of skinned.

rupert's next, and he's louder than all of us.

see you when you're ready,
maisy

---

## 3. Rupert Rubber — the sole and the connector
**Subject:** they told you i talk too much

rupert here. rubber. tapped straight from a tree, no oil, no factory sludge.

harry and maisy have probably warned you about me. "rupert's loud." "rupert never stops." rude. also
true.

here's why i talk. i'm the one holding your whole shoe together. the sole under your feet, the flex
when you walk, the grip on the pavement, the part that keeps the upper and the outsole from going
their separate ways. no me, no shoe. just a pile of nice ideas.

big sneaker replaced me with plastic and glue that outlives cathedrals. i'd rather come from a tree
and go back to the ground.

anyway. i've said plenty. cory's up next. you'll have to lean in, he's the quiet one.

hold tight,
rupert

---

## 4. Cory Coconut — the insole
**Subject:** you won't notice me, and that's the plan

hi. cory. coconut.

well, the husk. the bit everyone throws out once they've taken the good stuff. i'm what's left over.
turns out what's left over makes a very good insole. soft, breathable, a little spring in every step.

i'm underfoot, so you'll never see me and you'll rarely think of me. that's how i like it. i do my
best work when you've completely forgotten i'm there.

no oil in me. and nothing was grown to make me. i am the leftovers, just put to better use.

rupert said i'm the quiet one. rupert says a lot of things. flora's next, and she's older than all of
us. she barely raises her voice.

quietly yours,
cory

---

## 5. Flora Flax — the stitching
**Subject:** the oldest thread in your shoe

flora. flax.

people spin me into linen and think that's modern. i've been thread for around thirty thousand years.
i dressed pharaohs. now i stitch your shoes together and hold the top layer of your insole in place.
same job, new feet.

i don't chase seasons. i was never in fashion, so i can never fall out of it. strong, light, grown
from a small blue flower most people walk straight past.

let the others tell you they're the future. i'm the part that has always worked.

candy's next. she does all the little things the rest of us get away without doing.

steady as ever,
flora

---

## 6. Candy Cotton — the finishing
**Subject:** the parts you never think about, that's me

candy. cotton.

quick quiz. the laces you tied this morning. the lining your foot slides into. the small stitches, the
label at the tongue, the embroidery you liked in the photos. who do you think does all of that.

me. all of it. i'm the finishing. i'm everywhere you don't look. without me, a shoe is a sole and a
good intention.

grown, not drilled. soft against your skin, no plastic thread pretending to be me.

i won't keep you. i've got a thousand tiny things to hold together. eugene's next, and he'll tell you
how fast he grows. he always does.

in the details,
candy

---

## 7. Eugene Eucalypt — the soft part of the upper
**Subject:** i grow fast, drink little, leave the place better

eugene. eucalypt. pleasure.

let me get my numbers out of the way, because they're good and i'm proud of them. i grow fast. i
barely drink. i leave the soil better than i found it. and when they spin me into lyocell, i become
the soft, strong part of your upper, the bit that feels like nothing you've worn before.

closed-loop, too. almost everything used to make me gets captured and used again. next to nothing
wasted. i may have mentioned that. twice.

candy says i'm an overachiever. i prefer thorough.

one of us left. soy boy. he does the smallest job of all, and he'll be the proudest.

onward,
eugene

---

## 8. Soy Boy — the ink
**Subject:** i print the logo, that's my whole job, i love it

hi hi hi. soy boy here.

i have one job. i'm the ink. the little Nooch logo on your label and your insole. that's me. that's
all me. i printed it. did you see it. did you like it.

i'm made from soy, not solvents. i do the least harm i can, even when nobody's watching, which is
always, because i'm ink.

that's it. that's the whole introduction. i'm a very good boy, i love this shoe, and i love that
you're here.

right. the whole crew's introduced now. go grow a pair. or don't. but you should.

good boy out,
soy boy
